Forecast accuracy

How a forecast is made

λ = yellows/90shrunk × expected minutes/90 × referee × venue × derby
P(card) = 1 − e−λ

  • Blend — current-season counts plus last season capped at 900 minutes of weight (450 for Championship form), so live form takes over as the season runs.
  • Shrinkage — every player gets 900 pseudo-minutes at his position's league rate mixed in. Big samples keep their rate; one-minute cameos return the position mean instead of a 90-fouls-per-90 artefact.
  • Referee — the official's yellows per game over league average, clamped 0.70–1.40. TBC games use 1.00.
  • Venue & derby — home 0.95, away 1.08; listed rivalries add up to +18%.
  • Game state — when simulator win probabilities are supplied, likely-chasing underdogs get up to ×1.20 (tactical fouls) and dominant favourites down to ×0.85, shown as chase on the fixture card. Neutral without simulation data.

Before each matchweek the pipeline freezes every probability into a forecast log; after results land it scores them. The Brier score above is the mean squared error of those frozen forecasts (lower is better — 0.25 is coin-flip guessing on a 50/50 event; a well-calibrated card model typically sits near 0.15). The calibration table shows whether players priced at, say, 20–30% really get carded 20–30% of the time.

How to use the desk

A stats-based forecast desk for Premier League bookings markets. The Fixtures tab prices every player in every game of the matchweek — P(card) plus fair odds — so your per-fixture research starts priced, not blank. Players, Clubs and Referees are the underlying form tables. The Tracker logs and auto-settles picks; the Model tab reports how accurate the published forecasts have actually been.

Where the numbers come from

Player, club and referee form via ScoutingStats and public data. Pre-season, everything runs on 2025-26 form (promoted clubs on Championship form, flagged EFL). A weekly pipeline refresh pulls the new season's stats, fixtures and results after every matchday: the blend shifts to live form automatically, tracked picks settle, and the forecast log gets scored.

Suspension watch

The Premier League bans players at 5 yellows (through matchweek 19), 10 (through 32) and 15. The Fixtures tab flags anyone one booking short — a rest risk, and sometimes a player who suddenly tackles like a saint.

Known limits

  • Pre-season forecasts are last season's form; treat promoted-club numbers as indicative until real PL minutes arrive.
  • Expected minutes is a heuristic (minutes share pre-season, minutes per appearance in-season) until lineups drop. Once the lineup fetcher runs near kick-off, cards show XI confirmed: starters price at full minutes, bench players at cameo minutes, everyone else drops out.
  • The odds box on each candidate is your bookmaker's price; EV = odds × P − 1. Odds typed before logging a pick are saved with it, so the tracker records the price you actually saw.
  • Opponent style (how much a side draws fouls) ships neutral until that harvest is switched on; the wiring is already in place.
  • No probability is a guarantee. A 35% chance misses two times in three.
